GeoIntel Interactive

GeoIntel Interactive is a geopolitical intelligence platform that turns global affairs data into an explorable map-based interface. The project brings together international relations, economic indicators, political systems, demographics, conflicts, strategic assets, natural resources, critical infrastructure, world leaders, and emerging AI capabilities into one interactive environment. Users can switch between thematic views, compare countries, inspect bilateral relationships, and explore how geography, power, resources, and institutions overlap. It is designed as both a data visualization experiment and a prototype for a more intuitive way to study the world.

Iran Protest Map

The Iran Protest Map is an interactive incident-tracking platform built to document and visualize protest activity across Iran. It geolocates protest footage, organizes incidents by time and place, and uses timelines, charts, and source links to help users understand the scale and spread of unrest. The project distinguishes between verified and unverified material, while also presenting varying estimates for deaths, arrests, and internet shutdown effects. It combines open-source research, mapping, and visual analysis to make fragmented protest evidence more transparent and easier to navigate.

History of Iran Layered

History of Iran Layered reimagines Iranian history as a geological cross-section, with dynasties and eras stacked like sedimentary layers across time. Beginning with ancient Proto-Elamite civilization and continuing into the Islamic Republic, the project uses proportional visual layers to show the extraordinary depth, continuity, rupture, and recurrence within Iranian civilization. Users can zoom, pan, adjust labels, toggle flags, and compare overlapping periods of rule. It is part historical timeline, part digital heritage artwork, and part experiment in making long civilizational history physically intuitive.

Tech & Politics Timeline

The Tech & Politics Timeline explores how technological change and political order evolve beside each other. By placing major inventions, scientific breakthroughs, communications systems, weapons, institutions, revolutions, and governance milestones onto a shared historical timeline, the project visualizes the long relationship between innovation and political complexity. Its core idea is that technology does not simply advance society, it repeatedly forces political systems to adapt, lag behind, or break. The result is a broad interactive map of civilization's recurring struggle to govern new forms of power.

AI Animations

AI Animations is a collection of cinematic short-form videos focused on creating historically accurate glimpses into the past. Built with AI image generation, animation tools, editing software, sound, transitions, and visual storytelling, the project experiments with reconstructing historical worlds in a way that feels vivid, immersive, and visually grounded. Most pieces focus on Iranian civilization, history, and related cultural themes, blending research with atmosphere and spectacle. The project sits between digital art, public history, and AI filmmaking, using new tools to make the past feel alive again.

About

I build interactive digital projects that combine art, politics, history, data visualization, design, and AI-assisted software creation. My work focuses on turning complex ideas into explorable visual experiences, from geopolitical mapping tools and historical timelines to cinematic AI animations and experimental web interfaces.

These applications represent a journey into AI-driven storytelling and interactive web experiences. By leveraging modern web technologies and AI, software can increasingly be created on demand around specific ideas, questions, and creative visions. Instead of being limited to static articles, videos, or dashboards, new tools make it possible to build custom digital experiences that let users explore information directly.

This portfolio is an ongoing experiment in that new generation of software: using AI, code, design, and visual art to make research, history, and global affairs more intuitive, immersive, and alive. It is also a living workspace for testing how cinematic media, spatial interfaces, data, and historical imagination can merge into custom software that feels built around a specific idea rather than squeezed into a generic format.
